A Russian strike on a ship near the southern Ukrainian port city of Odesa killed at least 10 people, while Ukraine launched hundreds of drones to attack Moscow, a day after Kyiv was hit by one of the heaviest barrages of ballistic missiles of the war.

Russia struck a Guinea-Bissau-flagged ship carrying corn with a crew from India and Syria, Ukraine's seaports authority said on Monday. Officials said eight crew members had been rescued. Russian strikes in Odesa have now killed 28 people in the month of July alone, regional governor Oleh Kiper said. Russia's Defence Ministry said its forces had hit fuel reservoirs at Odesa's port.

Russia has stepped up its attacks on Ukraine, frequently using ballistic missiles, as Kyiv is currently short of Patriot interceptor munitions crucial to defend against them. In retaliation, Ukraine is intensifying its attacks on Russian territory.

Moscow Mayor Sergei Sobyanin said more than 400 "enemy drones" were launched at the Moscow region between 8:30 p.m. and 5:00 a.m. Most were intercepted by Russia's air defence systems, including 85 destroyed while approaching the capital. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said Ukraine struck Russian logistics facilities and an oil depot in the Moscow region more than 400 km from the Ukrainian border.

Regional governor Andrei Vorobyov said 10 people, including a child, were wounded, with the main consequences recorded in Podolsk, Domodedovo and the Odintsovo district. Ukrainian drones also struck seven Russian vessels in the Black Sea and Sea of Azov, including three tankers and four dry cargo ships, bringing the total number of vessels struck in July to 183.
