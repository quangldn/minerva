Thailand has lodged concerns with China over its recent delivery of tanks to Cambodia, citing the fragility of a ceasefire between the Southeast Asian neighbors following last year's deadly border clashes.

Thai Prime Minister Anutin Charnvirakul raised the concerns with Chinese President Xi Jinping in a meeting on Friday last week, Thai Minister of Foreign Affairs Sihasak Phuangketkeow told reporters during the Thai delegation's visit to China. "Our concern lies in Cambodia's reporting of the tank delivery. At this time, relations are delicate, and the ceasefire is fragile," Sihasak said.

The concerns came after Thai authorities last month said 39 Chinese T-59D battle tanks had been delivered to Cambodia as part of an existing joint military training agreement, adding that the hardware did not seem to have been moved to the border afterward.

Relations between Thailand and Cambodia have remained tense after a third ceasefire agreement at the end of last year, following days of clashes in July and December that ranked among the deadliest between the two countries. A compulsory UN settlement process for maritime disputes, initiated by Cambodia earlier this year, has further strained relations and made talks on land border demarcation difficult.

China has said it maintains "normal defense cooperation" with both countries and that such ties do not target any third party. Sihasak said Thailand appreciated that China recognized the importance of remaining neutral, but stressed that the border matters must be resolved bilaterally. Separately, China and Thailand agreed to set up a dialogue mechanism involving their foreign and defense ministers.
