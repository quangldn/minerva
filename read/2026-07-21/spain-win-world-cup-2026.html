Spain are crowned World Cup champions after a 1-0 win over Argentina in the 2026 final on Sunday, July 19, at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey. Ferran Torres scored the decisive goal in extra time, delivering Spain its second World Cup title, 16 years after its first triumph in 2010.

The match capped the largest FIFA World Cup in history, a 39-day, 104-match tournament co-hosted by the United States, Canada and Mexico that drew more than six million fans. Spain reached the final having conceded only one goal in seven games, beating Portugal, Belgium and France along the way.

Argentina, the defending champion led by 39-year-old Lionel Messi, was bidding to become the first country to win back-to-back titles since Brazil in 1958 and 1962. But Spain's young side, inspired by 19-year-old Lamine Yamal, suffocated Messi and Argentina, who were reduced to 10 men after Enzo Fernandez was sent off for a second bookable offence in stoppage time.

The final, watched by a global audience and attended by heads of state including U.S. President Donald Trump, Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney and Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum, marked the emergence of a new Spanish generation at the pinnacle of world football.
