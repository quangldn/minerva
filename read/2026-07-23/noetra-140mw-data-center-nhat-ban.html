Japan to develop physical AI capabilities with cross-industry initiative (Noetra 140MW data center)

Nguồn: Light Reading — Gigi Onag, Senior Editor APAC — 21/07/2026

Japan has strengthened its bid to develop its own sovereign AI and physical AI ecosystem through the newly formed Noetra, which started full-scale research and development last week armed with an initial government funding of 387.3 billion Japanese yen (US$2.4 billion) and investments from 44 of the country's largest companies.

Noetra, a cross-industry initiative involving Japan's manufacturing, electronics, automotive and technology sectors, is tasked with developing homegrown AI models, with a particular focus on physical AI and robotics. The company's core founders and major shareholders include SoftBank, the Sony Group, NEC and Honda Motor.

Hironobu Tamba, who led the development of SoftBank's large language model, has been chosen as the president and CEO of Noetra, and its R&D team consists of engineers from companies that have joined the consortium. They will work alongside researchers from the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST) and the Japanese AI startup Preferred Networks.

In addition to the $2.4 billion in initial funding provided for this fiscal year through March 31, 2027, the government reportedly plans to provide Noetra with up to JPY1 trillion ($6.15 billion) in financial aid over the next five years.

140MW data center

Noetra is also building a 140-megawatt data center that will be powered by 13,750 Vera CPUs and 27,500 Rubin GPUs from Nvidia. The data center is expected to be operational by June 2028 and will serve as an AI factory to develop Noetra's homegrown foundational AI models that drive AI agents, digital twins, robotics and other physical AI applications.

According to Nvidia, the project brings together the country's manufacturing expertise, real-world industrial data and global technology leaders to develop highly reliable multimodal foundation models for physical AI. "Japan invented modern manufacturing. Now, it is building the AI factories that will power the next industrial revolution," Nvidia founder and CEO Jensen Huang said in a statement.

The government plans to capture over 30% of the estimated JPY60 trillion ($368.71 billion) global robotics market by 2040.
