The U.S. military carried out a wave of airstrikes in Iran over the weekend as it announced that a third U.S. service member had been killed in military operations since Friday. The attacks marked the ninth consecutive day of bombing since peace talks between the two countries collapsed in a dispute over a memorandum of understanding focused on the Strait of Hormuz, the key oil shipping waterway.

U.S. Central Command said a service member was killed during a controlled detonation of an Iranian drone in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq, and that it had recovered unidentified remains at a base in Jordan where two military personnel were killed in an Iranian strike Friday. President Trump said the waves of strikes were "in honor" of the dead service members. "We hit them very hard again tonight," he told reporters after landing in Maryland from an appearance at the World Cup final.

The U.S. says the weekend deaths bring to 17 the number of military personnel now killed in the conflict with Iran. Iran says at least 3,000 Iranians, many of them civilians, have been killed since the U.S. and Israel began the war against Iran on Feb. 28.

Iran kept up its attacks Monday in retaliation for U.S. strikes that targeted bridges and electrical infrastructure in a southern Iranian province alongside the Strait of Hormuz. Iran had said it would respond in kind and over the weekend targeted electricity installations and water desalination plants in Kuwait, which hosts a large U.S. military presence, and also targeted Bahrain, home of the U.S. Navy's 5th Fleet.

On Monday, Iran said it disabled two oil tankers that had attempted to transit the Strait of Hormuz without its permission. Oil prices continued to rise, with benchmark crude topping more than $90 per barrel. Iran also said its missiles targeted U.S. military aircraft at the international airport in the resort city of Aqaba, Jordan.
