Taiwan eyes photonics-powered future with Chunghwa-Fujitsu collaboration

Nguồn: Light Reading — Gigi Onag, Senior Editor APAC — 22/07/2026

Chunghwa Telecom has formed a two-year strategic partnership with Fujitsu and its network subsidiary, 1Finity, to accelerate the deployment of all-photonics network (APN) technology and explore future applications of quantum technology in Taiwan.

This partnership primarily renews an earlier collaboration between Chunghwa Telecom and Fujitsu to develop the country's next-generation communications infrastructure using APN technology based on the IOWN (Innovative Optical and Wireless Network) concept developed and introduced by NTT in May 2019.

APN is a telecommunications network architecture that uses light rather than electricity to transmit and process data from end to end. APN eliminates the need to convert optical signals into electrical signals for routing, which drastically reduces latency, increases bandwidth and lowers power consumption.

Traditional networks constantly switch between light and electricity to route data. An APN, however, maintains an unbroken optical pathway, enabling data to travel at the speed of light. Since there is no processing delay from electrical conversions, APNs can reduce latency significantly compared to traditional networks.

The first collaboration between Chunghwa Telecom and Fujitsu took place from 2024 to 2026. The operator built a verification facility for APN technology, and Fujitsu used its APN knowledge from Japan, its home market, to help develop a customized plan for the early commercialization of APN technology in Taiwan.

Specifically, the three companies will use Chunghwa Telecom's existing submarine fiber-optic cable systems to conduct end-to-end data transmission tests of APN technology. They will also work on APN cloud interconnection, focusing their testing on connecting multiple physically separated data centers via low-latency optical links using 1Finity network solutions and Fujitsu hardware.

"We will promote the construction and expansion of high-capacity, low-latency, and low-power-consumption optical networks connecting distributed data centers. Furthermore, we will continue to advance essential communications infrastructure in Taiwan through these breakthrough technologies," 1Finity CEO Masaaki Moribayashi said in a statement.

The three companies will be working on quantum security, developing security frameworks tailored for distributed data networks. The research will focus on applying quantum cryptography and quantum-safe protocols to protect sensitive network control layers.

Why it matters

Taiwan is experiencing a massive spike in data center computing demands, heavily straining network capacity and energy infrastructure. Currently, Taiwan has 36 data centers with a total capacity of approximately 60MW. The government forecasts that future capacity generated from AI applications and data centers will increase six and a half times to 450MW over the next three years.

The collaboration among Chunghwa Telecom, Fujitsu, and 1Finity aims to validate APN technology in order to address the challenges posed by surging network power consumption and data traffic resulting from the rise of AI. The partnership targets commercial-scale validation of energy-efficient, low-latency and highly secure next-generation networks.
